RecipesJune 28, 2026

The Art of the Seven-Course Tasting

A tasting menu is a story told in seven chapters. Each course must arrive at exactly the right moment, building intensity without exhausting the palate. At Seven Wonders, we begin light and bright, move through richness, and resolve in something sweet and quiet.

The secret is contrast — temperature, texture and acidity woven so no two plates feel alike. A cool citrus course resets the tongue before the wagyu; a whisper of salt sharpens the dessert. Restraint is the hardest discipline in the kitchen, and the most rewarding.

Next time you dine with us, notice the pacing. It is engineered, course by course, to feel effortless.

PairingsJune 15, 2026

Pairing Florida Citrus with Old-World Wine

Florida's citrus is a gift: vivid, floral and endlessly versatile. Pairing it with structured European wines takes a careful hand — too much acid and the wine turns flat, too little and the dish disappears.

Our sommelier leans toward high-acid whites and delicate reds that echo the fruit rather than fight it. The result is a pairing that feels inevitable, as though the vine and the grove grew for one another.

SourcingMay 30, 2026

From Boat to Table in Under a Day

Freshness is not a marketing word for us — it is a schedule. Our seafood is landed by day-boats along the Florida coast and reaches our kitchen within hours, still carrying the cold of the water.

That speed lets us cook simply. A great scallop needs only heat, butter and salt. The less time between the boat and the plate, the less we have to do.

BarMay 12, 2026

Building the Ember Old Fashioned

Our signature cocktail begins with smoke. We char an orange peel tableside, releasing oils that cling to the glass. Smoked bourbon, a dash of apricot bitters and a single large cube do the rest.

It is a drink built on patience — stirred, never shaken, until the dilution is exactly right. The ember glow is more than a name; it is the first thing you taste.

KitchenApril 26, 2026

A Day in the Life of Our Pass

The pass is the heart of the kitchen — the last checkpoint before a plate reaches your table. Long before service, the day begins with stocks, sauces and the quiet ritual of prep.

By evening, the tempo rises. Tickets call, pans flare, and every plate is inspected under the light before it leaves. It is controlled chaos, and we would not trade it for anything.

PastryApril 9, 2026

Mastering the Soufflé That Never Falls

A soufflé is equal parts science and theatre. The batter must be folded gently to keep the air, the ramekin buttered upward so it can climb, and the oven left undisturbed.

Timing is everything — a soufflé waits for no one. We fire it to order so it arrives at your table still rising, dusted with gold and ready for a spoonful of warm caramel.
